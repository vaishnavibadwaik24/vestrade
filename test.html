Challenges in Developing a Real Estate Project

1

Land Identification and Acquisition

Finding high growth potential land, evaluating its suitability for development, and negotiating favourable purchase terms.

2

Due Diligence

Ensuring the land has a clear legal title, verifying ownership history, checking for encumbrances, zoning regulations, and all other legal and technical verifications before acquisition.

3

Feasibility and Planning

Conducting market research and aligning the project with zoning and regulatory requirements.

4

Legal Approvals and Compliance

Securing land use permits, environmental clearances, and managing legal documentation.

5

Funding and Financial Management

Arranging high initial capital and maintaining cash flow during development.

6

Infrastructure Development

Planning and implementing roads, utilities, and other essential infrastructure.

7

Construction Management

Overseeing contractors, ensuring quality, and managing timelines effectively.

8

Material Procurement and Cost Control

Sourcing materials, controlling costs, and addressing unexpected overruns.

9

Marketing and Sales

Creating effective strategies to attract buyers and managing pre-sales processes.

10

Regulatory Updates and Monitoring

Adapting to changing policies and ensuring ongoing compliance.

11

Project Handover

Delivering completed units on time and addressing customer concerns.

12

Risk Management

Mitigating risks related to market fluctuations, natural disasters, and political changes.

13

Technical Expertise

Requiring in-depth knowledge of design, construction, and project execution.

14

Time Commitment

Managing lengthy processes from land acquisition to project completion.
