Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

What is a Real Estate Investment Fund (REIF)?

A Real Estate Investment Fund (REIF) is a SEBI-regulated investment vehicle that allows investors to pool their money to invest in income-generating real estate assets. REIF offer fractional ownership of premium commercial properties and provide regular income through rent and potential capital appreciation.

How does a REIF differ from traditional REIT?

While both are regulated by SEBI, REIF focus on smaller-scale real estate projects, typically valued between ₹50 crore and ₹500 crore, making them more accessible to individual investors compared to traditional REIT that usually involve larger assets.

What are the benefits of investing in REIF?

REIF offer investors access to high-quality real estate investments with lower capital requirements, diversification, professional management, regular income distributions, and potential capital appreciation.

What is the minimum investment amount?

The minimum investment in a REIF is typically ₹10 lakh, with additional investments in multiples of ₹10 lakh, making it accessible to a broader range of investors.

How are REIF taxed?

Taxation on income and capital gains from REIF depends on the investor's profile and the nature of income. Generally, income distributions may be taxed at the investor's applicable slab rate, while capital gains tax rates depend on the holding period. It's advisable to consult a tax advisor for specific details.

How can I invest in a REIF?

Investors can participate in REIF through public offerings listed on stock exchanges. Units can be purchased during the initial offer period or traded on the secondary market, providing liquidity and flexibility.

Who manages the properties within a REIF?

A professional investment manager is appointed to handle the acquisition, management, and eventual sale of the properties within the REIF, ensuring optimal returns for investors.

Are REIF safe investments?

REIF are regulated by SEBI, ensuring transparency, compliance, and investor protection. However, like all investments, they carry certain risks, and it's essential to assess these before investing.

Can I exit my investment before the maturity of the REIF?

Yes, since REIF units are listed on stock exchanges, investors can sell their units in the secondary market, subject to market conditions and liquidity.

What types of properties do REIF invest in?

REIF typically invest in income-generating commercial properties, such as office buildings, retail spaces, and warehouses, providing investors with exposure to the real estate sector's growth.
